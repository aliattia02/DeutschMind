üìñWhat are German Articles?

In German, every noun has a grammatical gender: masculine, feminine, or neuter. The article tells you which gender the noun has.

Unlike English (which only has "the" and "a/an"), German has different articles for each gender.

üìóIndefinite Articles - ein, eine

Indefinite articles are like "a/an" in English. They refer to non-specific things.

Example: "I see a book" = any book, not a specific one.
